Readable Newspapers

The mainstream origin of readability can be traced back to journalism in the 1940's when publishers realized more readable articles increase newspaper circulation.

Shown here are the results of a selection of studies observing how newspapers have become more readable over time. This graph is a modified version of a readability index that translates numerical readability calculations to US school grade reading levels.

Readable Everything

The readability trend has been studied in numerous sources outside of newspapers including the New York Times best seller list and High School English Curriculums. Certain states even mandate that their insurance forms must meet minimum readability levels.

Readability is especially important on social media where the most popular posts are often highly readable.

Domain Exception

One exception to this trend is sources that require domain specific language like legal, business and scientific writing.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Grade: Needs Improvement

Scores on the nation's largest reading comprehension test have been on a downward trend for over 30 years.

Students Read Less

A large-scale national trend like this suggests a broader cultural change rather than localized failings with individual districts. The National Assessment of Educational Progress has studied correlations between student behavior and test performance and found that how much time a student spends reading for fun outside of the classroom has a strong correlation with how well the student will perform on the reading comprehension assessment.

Since 1984, reading outside of the classroom has also been steadily declining from where almost 1 out of every 3 students spent some time reading for fun in 1984 to under 1 out of every 5 reading for fun in 2012.

 

What You Read Matters

A global study by the Organization for Economic Development also found that students who regularly read from multiple mediums instead of a single medium perform better.

The strongest readers in this study include books in the mix of mediums. While there isn't a definitive reason for why books improve reading comprehension, it is suggested that books foster a deeper understanding of the subject matter and better prepare students for more focused reading as compared with other formats.

 

 

     

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Reading Comprehension

Reading comprehension is the process of reading text to understand its meaning.

Construction-Integration

Illustrated here is the comprehension process as viewed in the widely accepted construction-integration model. The blue areas represent the three levels of reading comprehension that work in parallel to derive meaning from text. The white areas indicate the start and end of this process with the written text at the bottom and understanding by the reader at the top.

Comprehension Process

Text and Understanding

Readability Impacts Construction

The first two levels of the comprehension model are the construction phase where the meaning of the text is constructed by defining words and establishing their relation to one another. Readability has the most impact on this phase as it is solely focused on the written words.

Integration With Prior Knowledge

The integration phase is when the meaning of the text is linked with existing knowledge. This is where the benefits of having a strong outside knowledge is realized. In comprehension assessments, advanced readers are able to illustrate connections to ideas not explicitly mentioned within the text.

 

 

 

 

Longform Challenges

As text increases in length, new challenges are presented in the comprehension process as additional relationships between ideas and words are defined. Longform text requires additional concentration to ensure the full meaning is captured.

Digital Skim Reading

Digital reading follows a skimming pattern that forms an "F" like shape as time goes on, for longer forms of text a lot of the text is ultimately not read. This method is very effective at finding information online but not as effective when it comes to reading longer forms of text. Some educators suspect this skim reading is carrying over to all forms of reading, not surprising given how screens have become the dominant medium for reading.
